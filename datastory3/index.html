Misconceptions about Shooting Deaths in the United States

by Emerson Max

Please begin reading the story to follow the interactive graphic

In light of recent tragedies in the United States involving guns, the topic of gun control and gun safety has been a widely debated issue.

The tragedy in Las Vegas in October of 2017, for example, led to a debate about the “bump stock” that shooter Stephen Paddock used to rain bullets on the Vegas crowd at a quicker rate.

The legal purchase of assault weapons became another discussion topic after the gruesome high school shooting in Parkland, Florida in February of this year. Nineteen-year-old Nikolas Cruz killed seventeen people using an Assault Rifle, and this rightfully led many to question whether a regular citizen, especially one so young, should be able to purchase such a weapon.

While shootings and tragedies like this seem to be an extremely prevalent thing in the United States, a lot of these feelings can be attributed to the media’s coverage of such events. News of mass shootings or school shootings dominate the news cycle for days and sometimes weeks after the fact.
